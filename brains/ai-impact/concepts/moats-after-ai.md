# Summary

When [the scarcity collapse](./scarcity-collapse.md) flattens the old fortifications, defensibility doesn't vanish — it relocates. Three places stand out:

1. **Proprietary data + human insight.** AI can scrape the public web, but it can't crawl a customer's private telemetry, a decade of niche workflow logs, or the empathy born of lived experience. Unique datasets paired with domain experts who ask the right questions form a knowledge asset no crawler can mimic.
2. **Community & trust.** People buy from people. Genuine relationships — user groups, open road-maps, transparent governance — build a social fortress: advocates become an unpaid sales force, and churn becomes reputationally costly for would-be switchers.
3. **Tangible presence.** Hardware, support crews, real-world certifications — anything you can physically touch or verify remains stubbornly hard to clone with bits alone. The tighter the integration between the digital layer and real-world reliability, the stickier the moat.

# Why it matters here

This is [value-based strategy](../../site/concepts/value-based-strategy.md) under new physics: value created and captured still decides who wins, but the sources of durable advantage move — and they are what decide how long a [blue ocean](./blue-ocean-strategy.md) stays blue. The third moat is home turf — the [security industry](../../site/concepts/security-industry.md) is precisely a tight weld of digital control and physical, certified, real-world reliability.

# Core idea

Moats don't disappear under AI; they move to what AI can't cheaply copy — private knowledge, human trust, physical reality.
