# Summary

[Kim & Mauborgne](../people/kim-and-mauborgne.md)'s framework: rather than fighting for share in bloody *red oceans* of head-to-head competition, create *blue oceans* — uncontested market space where the competition is irrelevant — through value innovation. Alongside [Playing to Win](../../site/concepts/playing-to-win.md)'s where-to-play question, it's one of the classic answers to where a product should compete.

# Revisited under AI

If an LLM can cluster an entire market's chatter in minutes and Copilot-class tools ship version 1.0 in days, then *everyone* can unearth unmet needs and follow you into open water: competitive barriers flatten, red oceans swell with fast copy-cats, and process-centric SaaS loses ground to generic agents automating the rote workflows that once justified whole product lines. Finding blue water still matters — but keeping it blue now depends on where scarcity has moved ([the scarcity collapse](./scarcity-collapse.md)) and which [moats still hold](./moats-after-ai.md).

From the article [AI Impact — Blue Ocean Strategy](https://davidjcrawford.github.io/Portfolio/writing/ai-impact-blue-ocean-strategy/).

# Core idea

Uncontested market space is still the prize — but AI shortens how long an ocean stays blue, so the moat matters more than the discovery.
