# Who

I'm David J Crawford. I've had three careers at depth: roughly a decade as a software engineer, roughly a decade as a UX and design leader, and now product management for AI at a New Zealand security technology company. The combination is the point — most commentary on AI products comes either from technologists blind to the experience layer, or from strategists who can't reason about how the thing is built. I've worked in all three.

# How I think

I'm a first-principles thinker — I want to understand *why* before adopting a practice, and I'm sceptical of methods (Agile, Lean, Design Thinking) applied as ritual rather than understood as tools. I lean heavily on [systems thinking](../concepts/systems-thinking.md) and [leverage points](../concepts/leverage-points.md) (Donella Meadows), on [strategy as a kernel](../concepts/good-strategy.md) (Richard Rumelt), and on a conviction that [the quality of the specification is the quality of the engineering](../concepts/engineering-is-not-construction.md). I keep my knowledge externalised in structured, machine-readable form rather than locked in my head — this brain is an instance of that.

# What I do now

I lead AI product work in the [security & access-control industry](../concepts/security-industry.md): how to put generative and agentic AI to work in a way that keeps people [safe](../concepts/safe.md) and [secure](../concepts/secure.md). Alongside that I do [university advisory work](../situations/school-of-transdisciplinary-design.md) (University of Waikato, School of Design and School of Computing) on transdisciplinary design and how to teach agentic development as a discipline.

# Why I write

To externalise thinking, force clarity, and bridge silos. The interesting question of this era isn't how capable the models are — it's how we *craft* [product](../concepts/product.md) with them, through [transdisciplinary design](../concepts/transdisciplinary-design.md), to navigate the [Nth-order effects](../concepts/nth-order-effects.md) of AI in a world [beset with change](../concepts/beset-with-change.md). See [The Through-Line](../concepts/the-through-line.md).
