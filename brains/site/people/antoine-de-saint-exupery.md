# Who

French aviator and writer (*Le Petit Prince*, and the memoir *Terre des hommes* / *Wind, Sand and Stars*, where the line appears). Not a designer, but he wrote the cleanest statement of design by subtraction there is.

# The line

“Perfection is achieved, not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away.” It's the same instinct as Rams' [*less, but better*](./dieter-rams.md), reached from the other direction: you arrive at the essential by removing, not by adding.

# Why he's here

A reminder that the work is often deletion — of features, of copy, of ornament — until only what matters is left. See [Less, but Better](../concepts/less-but-better.md) and [Curation](../concepts/curation.md).
