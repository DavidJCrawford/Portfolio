# Summary

A design ethos drawn from two people. [Dieter Rams](../people/dieter-rams.md): *less, but better*, and *good design is thorough down to the last detail*. [Antoine de Saint-Exupéry](../people/antoine-de-saint-exupery.md): *perfection is achieved when there is nothing left to take away*. Together they name one discipline from two sides — reduce a thing to what genuinely matters, and make what remains thorough and exact.

# Reduction is not absence

Restraint here is a positive choice, not a lack of effort. Removing the arbitrary is harder than adding, and it's what lets the remaining detail read clearly. It is the sibling of [curation](./curation.md) — deciding what matters and being accountable for it — applied to form rather than to options.

# In this site

The site is built this way on purpose: one accent colour, a single block fill, near-zero JavaScript, and [design tokens](../technical/design-tokens.md) as the only source of truth. The restraint is deliberate, not an accident, and it's why [product](./product.md) here is a craft rather than a pile of features.

# Core idea

Strip to what matters, then get that exactly right.
