# Summary

The habit of asking *why* before adopting a practice — and staying sceptical of methods (Agile, Lean, Design Thinking) applied as ritual rather than understood as tools. A framework is a compression of someone's reasoning; if you can't unpack it back to the problem it solves, you're performing it, not using it — and you won't know when it doesn't apply. It's how [David](../people/david-j-crawford.md) works, and how this brain treats every framework it holds: each note tries to carry the *why*, not just the recipe.

# Why it matters

It pairs with [systems thinking](./systems-thinking.md): first principles take you down to what's actually true; systems thinking builds back up to how it behaves in the world. Together they're the guard against cargo-culting — especially urgent when AI makes it trivially cheap to generate plausible-looking process.

# Core idea

Derive the method from the problem; never perform the method at the problem.
