# Summary

Once a model can generate many credible variants on demand, the bottleneck moves from making to *choosing*. The value shifts to curation: deciding what is best for a given context, and why — and being accountable for that decision. There is rarely a single “best” answer; there are many roughly-equivalent options, and the real skill is discrimination — knowing which differences actually matter for the outcome and which are decorative.

# A real discipline

Curation isn’t “just taste.” It’s a serious, formal practice (there are postgraduate programmes in curatorial practice). In an AI-heavy workflow it reframes what a senior designer is: not the person who produces the most, but the one who reliably chooses the right direction, for the right reasons, while keeping the [product](./product.md) coherent over time.

# Core idea

Don't chase the one perfect answer; generate strong options and curate well.
