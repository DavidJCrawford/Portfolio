# Summary

[Agentic AI](./agentic-ai.md) is crossing from *tool you drive* to *delegate that acts for you* — an agent that researches, buys, books, negotiates, files, and operates systems on a person's behalf. That is a **principal-agent** relationship, and it inherits all the old questions of delegation (does the agent act in *my* interest or its platform's? can I verify what it did? who is accountable when it errs?) at machine speed and machine scale.

# Why it matters

The first-order framing — "it can do tasks for me" — is the least interesting part. The stakes live in the [Nth-order effects](./nth-order-effects.md): trust and verification, liability, the erosion or amplification of human [agency](./wisdom-work.md), new attack surfaces (an agent is a new thing to phish, poison, or impersonate), market dynamics when both sides of a transaction are agents, and the concentration of power in whoever supplies the agents. Reasoning about that ripple needs [systems thinking](./systems-thinking.md) and the wide, cross-stakeholder view of [transdisciplinary design](./transdisciplinary-design.md) — not just a demo. It sits squarely on the *why* of this work: shape it so it keeps people [safe](./safe.md) and helps them [thrive](./helping-people-thrive.md).

Where an agent acts for a person in high-stakes settings, the [deterministic-vs-probabilistic](./deterministic-vs-probabilistic.md) discipline matters most: the agent proposes, but a deterministic, auditable engine should dispose. And an agent can only act well on someone's behalf if it can *understand* the world it acts in — which is the case for making knowledge legible ([knowledge as leverage](./knowledge-as-leverage.md); see [Optimise the Site for AI Agents](../decisions/optimise-for-ai-agents.md)).

# Open questions

An active line of inquiry, to develop over time — trace the Nth-order effects so the harms can be mitigated and the good maximised:

- **Alignment of interest** — how do we keep an agent loyal to the human, not to the platform that ships it?
- **Verification & accountability** — how does a person trust, audit, and stay liable for what their agent did?
- **Agency** — where does delegation free people for [wisdom work](./wisdom-work.md), and where does it quietly hollow out judgement and skill?
- **Systemic effects** — what happens to markets, institutions and trust when agents transact with agents at scale?

# Core idea

From *doing tasks* to *acting for you*. Ask not just "what can the agent do?" but "and then what — for the person it represents, and for everyone downstream?"
