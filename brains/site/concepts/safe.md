# Summary

Safety — protecting people from harm (physical, psychological, societal). Related to but distinct from [secure](./secure.md). Part of the *why* of the work: solutions should first keep people safe. Grounded in the [security industry](./security-industry.md) but far broader, and a key reason to keep [deterministic guarantees](./deterministic-vs-probabilistic.md) under any AI.

# The shape of harm

Harm is wider than injury. Physical: the failure modes of doors, alarms and evacuations. Psychological: people need to *feel* safe, and to be able to trust the systems watching over them. Societal: a deployed system's [Nth-order effects](./nth-order-effects.md) can erode the trust communities depend on long after its first-order metrics look fine. Keeping people safe means owning all three — and never resting a harm-carrying decision on a component that is only *probably* right.

# Core idea

Keep people safe — the first duty of the work.
