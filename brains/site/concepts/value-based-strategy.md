# Summary

[Felix Oberholzer-Gee](../people/felix-oberholzer-gee.md)'s framework (*Better, Simpler Strategy*, 2021): stop drowning in metrics and focus on one thing — value created — modelled with a *value stick*.

# The value stick

From top to bottom: **willingness-to-pay (WTP)**, price, cost, **willingness-to-sell (WTS)**. WTP is the most a customer would pay; WTS is the least employees and suppliers would accept. **Value created = WTP − WTS.** That gap splits three ways: customer delight (WTP − price), the firm's margin (price − cost), and satisfaction for employees and suppliers (cost − WTS).

# The move

You can win in only two ways: raise WTP (make the product something customers want more) or lower WTS (make the work more attractive or more efficient for the people and suppliers who provide it). Almost everything else is detail. The approach is data-driven and cascades neatly through an organisation.

# Why here

The [viability](./viability.md) lens made concrete: it names exactly *where* value comes from, and it pairs with [Playing to Win](./playing-to-win.md)'s *how will we win?* and [good strategy](./good-strategy.md)'s diagnosis. Further reading: [Value-Based Strategy](https://online.hbs.edu/blog/post/value-based-strategy) (HBS Online, Tim Stobierski).

# Core idea

Create value by raising willingness-to-pay or lowering willingness-to-sell; little else moves the needle.
