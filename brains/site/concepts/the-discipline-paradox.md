# Summary

The counter-intuitive lesson of working with [agentic AI](./agentic-ai.md) at pace: it demands *more* discipline, not less. Traditional development tolerated shortcuts because humans absorb ambiguity — unclear requirements get figured out along the way, inconsistent standards get adapted to, vague documentation gets filled in with institutional knowledge. Agents don't work that way. They need clarity, structure, well-defined interfaces and consistent patterns — so the shortcuts stop being free.

# The uplift

Held to that standard, the whole practice sharpens: requirement definitions get tighter than anyone thought necessary, documentation becomes load-bearing, interfaces get cleaned up. This is [engineering-is-not-construction](./engineering-is-not-construction.md) experienced from the inside — the specification *is* the engineering — and it's why [externalised knowledge](./knowledge-as-leverage.md) is the leverage point: it's what the agents actually consume. In practice: start with outcomes, not tasks — bring the AI in on day zero with the business case, so it holds the full context of the desired outcome rather than a fragment of it. The same clarity is what makes [guardrails and golden paths](./guardrails-and-golden-paths.md) followable at all — an unspecified golden path is just a wish.

Explored at length in [Lessons on AI Acceleration](https://davidjcrawford.github.io/Portfolio/writing/lessons-on-ai-acceleration/).

# Core idea

AI doesn't reward sloppiness with adaptability the way humans do — the price of acceleration is doing things well.
