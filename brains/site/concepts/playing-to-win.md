# Summary

[Roger Martin](../people/roger-martin.md) and A.G. Lafley's framework (*Playing to Win: How Strategy Really Works*, 2013): strategy is not a plan or a document but an integrated set of five choices — a *cascade*.

# The cascade

1. **Winning aspiration** — what winning actually looks like.
2. **Where to play** — the markets, segments and customers you will compete in.
3. **How to win** — how you will create value there: lower cost, or genuine differentiation.
4. **Capabilities** — the distinctive activities that must be in place to win.
5. **Management systems** — what builds, supports and measures the choices.

*Where to play* and *how to win* do the heavy lifting; the other three enable or check them. You toggle among the five rather than moving top-down once.

# Why here

It is the sharpest tool for the [viability](./viability.md) lens — strategy as choice. It sits alongside [Rumelt's kernel](./good-strategy.md) (diagnosis before solution) and [value-based strategy](./value-based-strategy.md) (where the value actually comes from).

# Core idea

Strategy is a cascade of choices; where to play and how to win carry it.
