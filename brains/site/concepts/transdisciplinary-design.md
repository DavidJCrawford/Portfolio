# Summary

Designing across and beyond disciplines — integrating knowledge from many fields (and from outside academia, with real-world stakeholders) to address complex, “wicked” problems, rather than staying inside any one discipline's frame. It's the lens, tools and techniques through which [product](./product.md) gets crafted here, and it's how you can reason about [Nth-order effects](./nth-order-effects.md): no single discipline sees the whole system.

# In practice

I do [advisory work with the University of Waikato](../situations/school-of-transdisciplinary-design.md) — across its School of Design and School of Computing — on transdisciplinary design and on how to teach agentic development as a discipline. It pairs naturally with [systems thinking](./systems-thinking.md): both insist that structure and relationships across domains matter more than any single part.

# Core idea

Transcend disciplinary silos to craft better solutions to complex problems.
