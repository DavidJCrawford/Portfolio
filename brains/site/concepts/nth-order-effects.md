# Summary

First-order effects are the obvious, immediate result of a change. Nth-order effects are everything that ripples out afterwards — the second-, third- and tenth-order consequences as the change propagates through a [system](./systems-thinking.md). They're where the real story usually is, and they're hard to predict precisely because systems are non-linear.

# Why this site exists

[Generative AI](./generative-ai.md) and [agentic AI](./agentic-ai.md) have enormous Nth-order effects — on the nature of work, on trust, on which skills matter, on institutions and on safety. The first-order “it writes code / makes images” is the least interesting part. Reasoning about the downstream effects is the work, and it takes the wide view of [transdisciplinary design](./transdisciplinary-design.md): zoom out to a longer horizon, a wider set of stakeholders, and the second-order consequences — not just the problem in front of you.

# Core idea

Ask not only “what does this do?” but “and then what?” — repeatedly.
