# Summary

A metaphor that reframes building software with AI. In civil and structural engineering, engineers don't pour the concrete — they produce specifications and a construction company builds from them. The spec is the primary deliverable. Software conflated the two roles for fifty years: the developer designs the solution *and* writes the code. [Agentic AI](./agentic-ai.md) now serves as the construction company, so for the first time software can — and must — separate engineering (writing the spec) from construction (writing the code).

# Why the spec becomes the leverage

When the engineer and the builder are the same person, a skilled developer silently compensates for a vague spec through intuition while coding. When agents build, that hidden compensation disappears: a vague spec yields vague, incomplete software, just as a vague blueprint yields a dangerous bridge. So the quality of the specification *is* the quality of the engineering — which is exactly why [knowledge and context](./knowledge-as-leverage.md) are the [leverage point](./leverage-points.md).

# Core idea

Understand construction so you can write buildable specs — but the spec is the craft.
