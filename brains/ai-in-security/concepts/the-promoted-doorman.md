# Summary

The article's answer to the fear that AI replaces security people. Eighty years ago large buildings employed doormen; automated access control made much of that work unnecessary and, in the same move, gave us campuses, hospitals and airports operating at a level of safety manual labour could never have delivered. Agentic AI follows the same arc.

# What changes

It removes the drudgery — the alarm triage, the log-trawling, the swivel-chair between six systems — and frees people for the judgement work humans are uniquely good at. The goal is not a building with nobody watching; it is a building where the people watching can finally see everything that matters. This is [wisdom work](../../site/concepts/wisdom-work.md) in a security setting, and part of how the work [helps people thrive](../../site/concepts/helping-people-thrive.md).

# Core idea

Automation promotes the human from drudgery to judgement. Explored at length in [The Right People, at the Right Time](https://davidjcrawford.github.io/Portfolio/writing/the-right-people-at-the-right-time/).
