# Summary

What “future-proof” actually means here. It is not picking the model with the best benchmark this quarter — that model will be obsolete before the building's fit-out is finished. It is choosing an architecture that lets you swap the intelligence layer freely while the [deterministic foundation](./two-layer-architecture.md) stays rock-solid beneath it.

# Platform, not gadget

It is also the line between a gadget and a platform: a clever camera is a gadget, while a platform is what is still serving you in a decade. Buildings that age well treat AI as an orchestration layer over a trustworthy core, keep humans in the loop where it counts, and stay honest about the difference between *clever* and *certain*. It is the [security-industry](../../site/concepts/security-industry.md) reading of [good strategy](../../site/concepts/good-strategy.md): choose the architecture, not the model of the moment.

# Core idea

Future-proof the architecture, not the model — keep the intelligence swappable and the foundation fixed.
